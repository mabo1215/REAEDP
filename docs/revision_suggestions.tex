\subsection*{Priority 6: Replace the Conclusion with a More Final TIFS-Style Version}

\subsubsection*{Current problem}
The conclusion still contains several outdated phrases:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \texttt{unified framework},
    \item \texttt{under the Wiener kernel space},
    \item \texttt{reproducible algorithm}.
\end{itemize}
These are still broader or less polished than needed.

\subsubsection*{Suggested replacement conclusion}
\begin{quote}
\texttt{
We have proposed REAEDP, a differential privacy framework for entropy-calibrated data release with formal guarantees and attack-based evaluation.
The main technical contributions are twofold:
first, an explicit sensitivity bound for histogram entropy (Theorem~3), which enables calibrated differentially private release of histogram statistics;
second, a synthetic-data release mechanism \(F\) with a formal \((\varepsilon,\delta)\)-DP guarantee under the stated parameter domain (Theorem~4).
Across multiple public tabular datasets, the experiments show that the empirical entropy sensitivity remains below the theoretical bound in the tested regime,
that standard Laplace and Gaussian histogram baselines achieve comparable behavior,
and that membership-inference and linkage-style attack performance moves toward random-guess behavior as \(\varepsilon\) decreases.
These results support the use of REAEDP as an algorithmic release pipeline for privacy-preserving data publication in the tested settings.

This work also has several limitations.
The entropy-based release depends on histogram discretization and may be sensitive to bin selection, especially in high-dimensional settings.
Scalability to very high-dimensional or very large-scale data may require approximate or distributed implementations.
The RKHS/Wiener-kernel analysis relies on finite-dimensional numerical approximation of an infinite-dimensional space,
and tightening the associated approximation error under differential privacy remains open.
Finally, while the paper explicitly evaluates membership inference and a linkage-style attack,
stronger experimental coverage of attribute inference and broader multimodal or federated settings is left for future work.
}
\end{quote}
