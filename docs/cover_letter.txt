Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript titled "REAEDP: Entropy-Calibrated Differentially Private Data Release with Formal Guarantees and Attack-Based Evaluation" for consideration for publication in IEEE Transactions on Information Forensics and Security (TIFS).

Scope. The manuscript presents (i) an entropy sensitivity bound for histogram statistics (Theorem 3) that enables calibrated differentially private release of histogram entropy, and (ii) a synthetic-data mechanism with a formal differential privacy guarantee under stated conditions (Theorem 4). It further includes baseline comparison, parameter studies, and attack-based evaluation (membership inference and linkage-style) on public tabular datasets. The experimental results support the framework in the tested setting: empirical entropy change remains below the theoretical bound, and attack performance moves toward random-guess behavior as the privacy parameter decreases.

Fit with TIFS. The manuscript fits TIFS because it studies privacy-preserving data release, information-theoretic sensitivity analysis, and attack-based evaluation of output-side inference risks. It combines theorem-level formal guarantees with empirical validation and algorithmic implementation.

We confirm that the manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration by another journal. All authors have approved the manuscript and agree with its submission to TIFS.

Sincerely,
The Authors

ANSWERS TO EDITORIAL QUESTIONS (each answer: one paragraph, under 500 words)
---

1. Explain in detail why the contribution of this manuscript is within the scope of the IEEE Transactions on Information Forensics & Security? (max 500 words)

The manuscript is within TIFS scope because it addresses output-side inference risks (membership inference and linkage) when releasing statistics or synthetic data, provides formal differential-privacy guarantees with entropy-calibrated noise, and combines sensitivity analysis with attack-based evaluation, aligning with the journal’s focus on information security and forensics.

---

2. Why is the contribution significant (What impact will it have)? (max 500 words)

The contribution is significant because it gives the first explicit entropy sensitivity bound for histogram release enabling calibrated DP, specifies a synthetic-data mechanism with (ε,δ)-DP at theorem level, and links theory to practice through baseline comparison and MIA/linkage attack evaluation on public datasets.

4. What is distinctive/new about the current paper relative to these previously published works? (max 500 words)

Relative to Dwork and Roth we add entropy sensitivity bounds and a concrete (ε,δ)-DP synthetic-data mechanism with attack evaluation; relative to Dwork et al. on calibrating noise we extend calibration to histogram entropy and combine it with mechanism F and attack evaluation; relative to Song et al. we focus on non-interactive release and entropy calibration rather than training-time privacy.
